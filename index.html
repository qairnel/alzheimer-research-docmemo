Understanding the Memory Questionnaire

‚ñº

The DocMemo questionnaire evaluates lifestyle, medical history, family background and functional changes using validated tools such as the CAIDE risk score and the Cognitive Function Instrument (CFI). These scientifically recognized questionnaires are used in Alzheimer‚Äôs prevention research and help determine whether a medical consultation or memory assessment may be useful. The test takes about 10 minutes and is fully anonymous. At the end of the assessment, you receive personal guidance that you can share with your healthcare provider.

If your answers suggest that participation in a clinical study might be appropriate, we help connect you with nearby research sites. Participation is entirely voluntary, and your information is not shared with research centers unless you choose to contact them.

Who Should Consider Taking the Test?

‚ñº

The memory questionnaire is designed for adults aged 45+ who:

  • Notice recent changes in memory or daily functioning
  • Have a parent or family history of Alzheimer‚Äôs disease
  • Want to check eligibility for ongoing clinical trials
  • Care for a spouse or parent showing early signs of cognitive change

The questionnaire is designed for individuals and families seeking clarity.

Memory loss does not always indicate Alzheimer‚Äôs disease. Stress, sleep, medications and other conditions can affect memory. The goal of the questionnaire is not to give a diagnosis but to provide clarity, reduce uncertainty and offer next steps. If needed, the tool guides you toward professional evaluation or relevant clinical research opportunities.

A Supportive Approach for Families

‚ñº

Memory changes can be confusing and often worrying. DocMemo aims to offer reassurance, structure and actionable next steps. Your responses help identify whether a medical evaluation is recommended, and whether you might match current Alzheimer‚Äôs clinical trials.

Because Alzheimer‚Äôs disease progresses slowly and signs may appear long before diagnosis, early screening allows families to plan ahead, access emerging treatments, and consider participation in research that may help future generations.
